what it does

Two tools, one on-device engine.

A system-wide dictation tool and a meeting notetaker, sharing the same local transcription pipeline and the same understanding of your world.

🔒

Everything stays on your Mac

Speech-to-text, speaker separation, cleanup and summaries all run locally. No upload, no accounts, no analytics.

🛡️

Records the meeting, not your screen

Captures the other side of a call with a Core Audio process tap that needs only the narrow "System Audio Recording" consent. It never asks for Screen Recording.

🧭

Knows you from the room

Your mic and the call are captured as separate channels, so your voice is labeled "You" deterministically. It's never merged into a remote speaker, even when you talk over each other.

👥

Scales to 9+ speakers

A clustering diarizer with no fixed cap. A nine-person roundtable becomes nine speakers, not four fixed slots to squeeze into. Tunable, with a confidence signal per turn.

🎙️

Dictate anywhere

Press your hotkey, speak, and polished text is typed at your cursor in any app with a live waveform so you know it's working. Or leave ambient mode on and just talk.

📝

Summaries that fit the meeting

Auto-detects 1:1 / standup / general and uses a matching template. Saved as Markdown.

🧠

Knows your world

A plain-text file teaches Better Voice your names, jargon, and acronyms. It's used in cleanup and summaries, so it gets them right the first time.

🎛️

Your models, your choice

Point it at local Ollama or any OpenAI-compatible endpoint. No vendor lock.

🛰️

Remote Voice

Press a hotkey on your Windows PC, speak, and the audio travels over your private Tailscale network to the Mac and lands at the cursor.

✨

Onboards & auto-updates

Lives in the menu bar with a guided first-run setup and in-app auto-updates

getting started

Up and running in a minute.

# 1 · install — drag Better Voice to /Applications
$ open BetterVoice.dmg

# 2 · clear Gatekeeper (self-signed build)
$ xattr -cr /Applications/BetterVoice.app

# 3 · a local model for polish + summaries (optional but recommended)
$ ollama pull qwen3.5:4b-mlx

# 4 · launch — the welcome screen sets up permissions, hotkey, and context

Requires macOS 26+ and Apple Silicon. See the Ollama guide for setup. Also works with LM Studio, etc...

privacy

Your voice never leaves your Mac.

Recordings are transcribed on-device. Cleanup and summaries talk only to the local or self-hosted endpoint you configure. Local history and audio files are debugging artifacts you can auto-delete.

  • No cloud account, no upload
  • No analytics or telemetry
  • Audio-only capture, never your screen
