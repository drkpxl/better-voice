why better voice

Private by architecture, not by policy.

Otter, Fireflies, Fathom and the cloud tiers of newer apps stream your meeting audio to their servers. For anyone discussing customers, finances, health, legal, or unreleased work, that's a non-starter. Same workflow — nothing leaves the machine.

what it does

Two tools, one on-device engine.

A system-wide dictation tool and a meeting notetaker, sharing the same local transcription pipeline and the same understanding of your world.

🔒

Everything stays on your Mac

Speech-to-text, speaker separation, cleanup and summaries all run locally. No upload, no accounts, no analytics.

🛡️

Records the meeting, not your screen

Captures the other side of a call with a Core Audio process tap — the narrow "System Audio Recording" consent. Never asks for Screen Recording.

🎙️

Dictate anywhere

Press your hotkey, speak, and polished text is typed at your cursor in any app — with a live waveform so you know it's listening.

📝

Summaries that fit the meeting

Auto-detects 1:1 / standup / general and uses a matching template — decisions, action items, the points that matter. Saved as Markdown.

🧠

Knows your world

A plain-text file teaches it your names, jargon, and acronyms — used in cleanup and summaries, so it gets them right the first time.

🎛️

Your models, your choice

Point it at local Ollama or any OpenAI-compatible endpoint — a small model for snappy cleanup, a bigger one for richer summaries. No vendor lock.

🛰️

Remote Voice

Press a hotkey on your Windows PC, speak, and the audio travels over your private Tailscale network to the Mac and lands at the cursor.

✨

Onboards & updates like a real product

Lives in the menu bar with a guided first-run setup and in-app auto-updates — check on launch, see what changed, restart to update.

on the roadmap

Where the speaker engine is headed.

Honest about what's landed and what's next. The foundation — per-speaker voice embeddings — is being built now.

in progress

You vs. the room

Mic and call audio captured as separate channels — "you" is known deterministically, and cross-talk degrades gracefully instead of scrambling the transcript.

in progress

Unbounded speakers

A clustering diarizer with no fixed cap — a 9-person roundtable becomes 9 speakers, each with a real voice embedding and confidence signal.

roadmap

Speaker fingerprinting

Recognize the same voice across meetings — "Speaker 3" today is named once and remembered next week. Local, no subscription.

roadmap

Agent access (MCP / CLI)

Let Claude Code, Cursor, or Codex search your transcripts and trigger dictation — your voice history as a local tool for your agents.

getting started

Up and running in a minute.

# 1 · install — drag Better Voice to /Applications
$ open BetterVoice.dmg

# 2 · clear Gatekeeper (self-signed build)
$ xattr -cr /Applications/BetterVoice.app

# 3 · a local model for polish + summaries (optional but recommended)
$ ollama pull qwen3.5:4b-mlx

# 4 · launch — the welcome screen sets up permissions, hotkey, and context

Requires macOS 26+ and Apple Silicon. Polish is optional — raw transcription works without a model server. See the Ollama guide.

privacy

Your voice never leaves your Mac.

Recordings are transcribed on-device. Cleanup and summaries talk only to the local or self-hosted endpoint you configure. Local history and audio files are debugging artifacts you can auto-delete.

  • On-device transcription & diarization
  • No cloud account, no upload
  • No analytics or telemetry
  • Audio-only capture — never your screen
  • Open source (MIT)
